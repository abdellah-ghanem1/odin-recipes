The Morroocan Zaalouk

Description

Zaalouk is a popular Moroccan cooked salad of eggplants and tomatoes seasoned with paprika, cumin, garlic, and herbs. Roasting the eggplants is an optional but recommended step for adding a layer of smoky flavor.

Ingredients

  • 12 - 14 oz eggplant, charred (approx. 2 lg)
  • ½ oz extra virgin olive oil, plus extra to grease
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 14 ½ oz canned diced tomatoes, drained
  • 1 ½ tsp paprika
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • ½ tsp salt, to taste
  • ⅛ tsp ground black pepper, to taste
  • ½ tsp lemon juice
  • parsley leaves, to garnish
  • cilantro leaves, to garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare the Eggplant and Tomatoes: Roast or grill 2-3 eggplants until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft. Simultaneously, roast 2-3 tomatoes until the skin blisters and can be easily peeled.
  2. Peel and Mash: Allow the roasted eggplants and tomatoes to cool slightly, then peel the charred skin from both. Afterward, mash the peeled eggplants and tomatoes together in a bowl.
  3. Cook with Spices: Heat some olive oil in a pan and add 2-3 minced garlic cloves along with Moroccan spices like cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper to taste. Stir in the mashed eggplant and tomato mixture.
  4. Simmer and Blend: Let the mixture simmer for approximately 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the mixture to thicken. Then, use a blender or a hand blender to blend the mixture to your preferred consistency.
  5. Finish and Serve: Season the blended mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro and a drizzle of olive oil. Serve your Moroccan Zaalouk as a dip or side dish with bread or crackers.
